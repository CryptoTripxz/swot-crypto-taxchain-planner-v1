🎓 UK Crypto Tax — For Dummies
10 key lessons · No jargon · No stress
⚡ 0 XP
0 / 10 complete
₿
1. Does HMRC know about my crypto?
Beginner · 2 min
▼
Yes — very much so. Since 2020, HMRC receives data directly from UK crypto exchanges (Coinbase, Binance UK, Kraken) under data-sharing agreements. They know your name, address, and transaction history.
⚠️ Warning
Deliberate non-disclosure can attract penalties up to 200% of the tax owed, plus interest.
HMRC treats crypto as a capital asset — exactly like shares or property. This has been their position since 2018 (HMRC Cryptoassets Manual CRYPTO10000).
💰
2. Capital Gains Tax (CGT) simply explained
Beginner · 3 min
▼
CGT = what you sold for minus what you paid (including fees). The first £3,000 of gains each tax year is completely free (Annual Exempt Amount). 2025/26 CGT rates: 10% (basic rate taxpayer) or 20% (higher rate).
💡 Key Tip
Swapping BTC → ETH feels like "staying in crypto" but HMRC counts it as: selling BTC (CGT event) AND buying ETH (new cost basis). Thousands miss this!
🏊
3. The Section 104 Pool explained
Intermediate · 3 min
▼
All your BTC purchases go into one blended pool at an average cost. When you sell, HMRC uses that blended average — you can't cherry-pick "the cheap ones". HMRC's priority: 1) Same-day rule, 2) 30-day rule, 3) Section 104 Pool.
🛏️
4. The 30-Day Bed & Breakfast Rule
Intermediate · 3 min
▼
If you sell crypto at a loss then buy the same coin back within 30 days, HMRC denies the loss claim — matching the sale against those new purchases instead.
⚠️ The Rule
Want to genuinely harvest a loss? Wait 31+ days before buying the same coin back.
⛏️
5. Staking, Mining & Income Tax
Intermediate · 3 min
▼
Earning crypto (staking, mining, airdrops) is taxed twice: Income Tax on the GBP value when you receive it, then CGT on any gain when you later sell. Don't miss staking income in your Self Assessment — it's taxable at receipt, not at sale.
🖼️
6. NFTs, DeFi & Edge Cases
Intermediate · 3 min
▼
NFTs are unique cryptoassets — same CGT rules apply. DeFi is complex: providing liquidity is likely a CGT disposal; yield farming rewards are income tax. Not a disposal: moving crypto between your own wallets, or gifting to a spouse (they take your cost basis).
📁
7. Record Keeping & How to Report
Beginner · 2 min
▼
You need a Self Assessment (SA100 + SA108) if gains exceed £3,000 or proceeds exceed £50,000. Key deadlines: Tax year ends 5 April. Online submission deadline: 31 January following year. TaxChain automates GBP valuation, pool calculations, and generates your SA100 with blockchain proof.
📅 Don't miss!
Late filing = £100 fine + interest. TaxChain generates your full submission in one tap.
🎯
8. CGT Planning Strategies
Advanced · 4 min
▼
Spousal transfers: CGT-free transfers between spouses; each gets their own £3,000 exempt amount = £6,000 combined. Loss harvesting: sell losing positions before 5 April to offset gains (wait 31 days to rebuy). Pension contributions: reduce adjusted income to shift crypto gains from 20% → 10% CGT rate.
🚨
9. The 7 Most Expensive Mistakes
Must-Read · 4 min
▼
❌ #1: "HMRC won't find out"
Exchange data has been shared since 2020. This costs people thousands.
❌ #2: Forgetting crypto-to-crypto trades
Every BTC → ETH swap is a CGT event. Most people only count GBP withdrawals.
⚠️ #3: Using FIFO instead of Pool method
HMRC requires Section 104 pool averaging. FIFO/LIFO are incorrect under UK law.
⚠️ #4: Not reporting staking income
Staking rewards are income at receipt — not when you sell.
⚠️ #5: Ignoring 30-day B&B rule
Selling at a loss and rebuying within 30 days destroys your loss claim.
📋 #6: Poor record keeping
Without GBP values at transaction time, HMRC can treat the full proceeds as a gain.
📋 #7: Missing £50k proceeds threshold
Even zero-gain filers must report if total proceeds exceeded £50k.
📋
10. Year-End Tax Checklist
Practical · 3 min
▼
📅 These expire 5 April
Once the tax year closes, these opportunities are gone forever.
1
Use your £3,000 CGT exempt amount — it doesn't roll over.
2
Harvest crypto losses — sell before 5 April, wait 31 days to rebuy.
3
Top up your ISA — £20,000 limit, resets 6 April. Tax-free growth forever.
4
Make pension contributions — reduces income, can drop CGT rate from 20% to 10%.
5
Transfer assets to spouse — free up their £3k exempt amount, use their lower rate.
